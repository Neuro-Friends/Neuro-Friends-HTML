Social Media Policy

Last updated: 11/04/2025

General Guidelines

  • Be Kind, Be You – Represent NeuroFriends in a positive, respectful way. No need to be fake-perfect—just be your awesome self while respecting others.
  • Respect Privacy – Don’t share personal stories, names, or details about others unless you have their clear permission.
  • No Online Drama Llamas – If you’ve got an issue, talk to us directly rather than airing it online.

Photo & Video Use

  • Consent is Cool (and Required)
    • We always ask for permission before taking or sharing identifiable photos or videos.
    • A no is a no. Always.
  • Opt-Out Options
    • If you don't want to be photographed or filmed, let us know—we’ll make sure you’re not included.
    • We may use stickers, lanyards, or other signals to indicate photo preferences.
  • Volunteer Responsibilities
    • Volunteers posting to social media must double-check consent before posting.
    • Never post photos of minors without signed parent/guardian permission.

Social Media Content

  • Do Share: Encouraging posts, creative content, event updates, and snack pics (with consent).
  • Don’t Share: Anything private, discriminatory, or that could "out" someone’s neurodivergent identity.

Privacy & Safety

  • Never share personal contact info or private group details.
  • If you're unsure whether you can tag or mention someone, ask them privately first.

If Something Goes Wrong

If you see a post or photo that you're uncomfortable with, please contact a facilitator or volunteer privately. We'll fix it quickly and with kindness—no drama, no shame.
